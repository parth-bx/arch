Urban Void

Academic Project 2024 Housing Cluster
01 — Concept

Light, Density & the Space Between

Urban Void interrogates the relationship between housing density and the psychological need for open sky. Situated in a rapidly densifying inner-city fragment, the project proposes a cluster of residential towers that carve deliberate voids — courtyards, terraces, and sky-bridges — to restore a sense of commons within compact living.

The guiding question: Can density feel generous? The answer arrives through subtraction — removing mass strategically to create light shafts, programme-rich voids, and shared thresholds that make density liveable.

02 — Design Process

Iteration Through Model & Section

The design process began with physical cardboard massing studies — 40+ iterations testing different void configurations. Sections became the primary drawing format: reading light penetration, sight lines, and the relationship between private balconies and shared walkways.

03 — Final Outcome

A Cluster That Breathes

The final scheme proposes four residential bars arranged in a pinwheel organisation, releasing a central courtyard garden accessible to all residents. Roof terraces cascade down at different levels, each oriented to capture afternoon sun.

04 — My Role

Individual Thesis Studio, 5th Year

What I did

  • Conducted site analysis — solar path, Nolli mapping, movement patterns
  • Developed 40+ cardboard massing models across three design phases
  • Produced all architectural drawings: plans, sections, elevations, details
  • Created 3D renders using Rhino + V-Ray, post-processed in Photoshop
  • Presented the project in intermediate and final jury reviews
  • Wrote the full project narrative and concept booklet
