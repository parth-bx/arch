Canopy

Academic Project2023Public Pavilion
01 — Concept

A Roof Without Walls

Canopy proposes the simplest architectural gesture: a roof. Designed for a neighbourhood park in South Delhi, the pavilion offers shelter without enclosure — a canopy that marks a place without claiming it, that invites without prescribing use.

The structure is assembled from modular steel components that can be disassembled and relocated — a meditation on impermanence and the temporality of public space.

02 — Design Process

Structure IS the Architecture

The process was a study in structural honesty. I worked through multiple structural strategies — tensile, folded plate, and space frame — before settling on a simple moment-frame grid. The decision was driven by buildability and visual simplicity.
